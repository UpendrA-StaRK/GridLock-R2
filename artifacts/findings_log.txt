SOURCE 1: arXiv:2505.06818 тАФ "Deep Learning for On-Street Parking Violation Prediction"
  Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.06818
  Approach: LSTM/deep learning on Thessaloniki on-street parking sensor data.
            Data augmentation + smoothing for missing/noisy sensor data.
  What worked in production:
    - Fine-grained spatial unit (parking sectors, not city-wide zones)
    - Sequence models to capture temporal autocorrelation
    - Data augmentation to handle sensor outages
  What NOT to copy:
    - Requires physical parking sensors тАФ not available here
    - DL model adds complexity without clear benefit when data is sparse
    - Their dataset is fundamentally different (occupancy, not violations)
  Applicable to current project:
    - The principle of fine-grained spatial units is already done (DBSCAN zones)
    - Data smoothing: rolling_7d_count already does this
  Overall verdict: LOW applicability. Dataset and problem formulation differ.

SOURCE 2: Research Survey тАФ DBSCAN + KDE for Crime/Traffic Hotspot Enforcement
  Link: mdpi.com, jadara.edu.jo (multiple sources)
  Approach: DBSCAN тЖТ cluster identity; KDE тЖТ density surface; weighted "danger score"
            per cluster; predict using Random Forest/regression; network routing.
  What worked in production:
    - HDBSCAN preferred over DBSCAN (handles varying density, no rigid eps)
    - Prediction Accuracy Index (PAI) = hit_rate / area_fraction тАФ primary validation metric
    - Rolling horizon evaluation (don't evaluate on one fixed test window)
    - Weighted danger score combining frequency + severity + time-decay
  What NOT to copy:
    - Network-based KDE (NKDE) requires road network data (external, prohibited)
    - Monte Carlo significance testing тАФ overly complex for hackathon
    - Feedback loop concern (over-policing bias) тАФ valid ethically but out of scope here
  Applicable ideas for current project:
    - PAI metric: computable from current outputs. Measures what fraction of actual
      violations in the test period fall within the predicted top-K zones.
      PAI = (violations_captured / total_test_violations) / (zone_area / total_area)
      This would be a STRONG additional metric for judge Q&A.
    - Time-decay weight: older violations matter less than recent ones.
      CIS could incorporate a recency weight (exponential decay over training window).
      But: formula change requires user approval per AGENTS.md.
    - HDBSCAN over DBSCAN: more robust. But: would require re-clustering from scratch.
      Risky to change now; flag as future work.

SOURCE 3: GitHub тАФ tadowney/ticket_analysis (NYC Parking)
  Link: github.com/tadowney/ticket_analysis
  Approach: NYC parking tickets тЖТ DNN to predict violation type/location.
            Revenue optimization framing.
  What worked: Zone-level aggregation before modeling (same as current approach).

SOURCE 4: GitHub тАФ nikgeokar/parking_stgcn (Spatio-Temporal GCN)
  Link: github.com/nikgeokar/parking_stgcn
  Approach: STGCN (Spatio-Temporal Graph Conv Net) тАФ models spatial adjacency of
            zones AND temporal dynamics simultaneously.
  What worked: Captures inter-zone spatial dependencies (zone 2 adjacent to zone 5
               тЖТ violations spill over). GCN outperforms tabular ML on this.
  What NOT to copy: Requires PyTorch Geometric, much higher complexity.
                    Training time would exceed demo constraints.
                    Dataset too small (140 zones, 150 days) for GCN to generalize.
  Applicable: The concept of spatial adjacency between zones is interesting but NOT
              implementable within current architecture constraints. Flag as future work.

SOURCE 6: Cyclical Encoding of Hour/Day Features (multiple sources)
  Links: arxiv.org, towardsdatascience.com, stackexchange.com
  Approach: sin(2╧А*hour/24) and cos(2╧А*hour/24) replace raw integer hour_of_day.
            Fixes "midnight paradox" тАФ hour 23 and hour 0 appear adjacent to the model.
  What worked: Measurable RMSE improvements in traffic/energy prediction studies.
               XGBoost converges faster; better generalization on boundary hours.
  What NOT to copy: For tree-based models, impact is smaller than for neural networks.
                    XGBoost can handle non-monotonic relationships via splits.
  Applicable to current project:
    - SHAP Gate 3 FAILED: hour_of_day is only rank 6 in importance.
    - Cyclical encoding could increase hour_of_day's discriminating power.
    - Risk: LOW. Adding 2 columns (hour_sin, hour_cos) with no removals.
    - Would require retrain + re-run 06_shap.ipynb to verify gate improvement.
    - Impact estimate: MEDIUM. May improve per-hour NDCG (currently 0.890).
    - This is the most directly applicable research finding.

SOURCE 7: EPS Weighted Formula тАФ Enforcement Priority Score
  Links: spatialanalysisetc.com, urbansdk.com
  Approach: EPS = w1*C + w2*V + w3*J (weighted sum, not product like current CIS)
            Time-decay factor: older violations get exponentially lower weight.
            Normalized 0-1 scale for each component.
  What worked:
    - Additive formula more interpretable than multiplicative for judges
    - Time-decay improves relevance of recent enforcement data
    - Normalization lets you explain weight contributions in judge Q&A
  What NOT to copy: Changing CIS formula mid-project requires version control
                    per AGENTS.md. Would need user approval.
  Applicable:
    - The normalization insight is useful: CIS scores are currently not
      normalized (cis_score range is [0.0, X.XX]). Adding a 0-1 normalization
      step in cis_table would make the formula more interpretable.
    - This is a SAFE change (additive transformation, no model retrain needed).

SOURCE 8: PAI тАФ Prediction Accuracy Index
  Links: researchgate.net, nih.gov, mdpi.com
  Formula: PAI = (% test violations in predicted top-K zones) / (% area covered by top-K zones)
  What worked: Standard spatial validation metric used by police analytics worldwide.
               Directly answers "does your model identify the right places?"
  What NOT to copy: Monte Carlo significance testing тАФ not needed for demo.
  Applicable:
    - Computing PAI for GridLock R2 is DIRECTLY ACHIEVABLE from existing outputs.
    - Requires: test violation counts per zone (already in eval JSON) +
                zone area estimate (lat/lon bounding box per zone from clustering).
    - This would be a COMPELLING additional metric for demo scorecard:
      "Our model covers X% of the city's area but captures Y% of all violations"
    - Impact: HIGH for demo credibility with the Bengaluru Traffic Police judges.
    - Risk: LOW тАФ additive to metrics.py, no retrain needed.
